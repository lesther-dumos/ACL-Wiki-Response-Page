ACL Tears in Female Athletes: Q&A with a Sports Medicine Expert

Females are more prone to ACL tears due to a combination of anatomical, hormonal, and biomechanical factors. Women typically have a wider pelvis, which creates a different angle between the thigh bone and shin bone, increasing stress on the ACL. Hormonal fluctuations, like estrogen, may affect ligament laxity. Moreover, differences in muscle strength, neuromuscular control, and landing mechanics can lead to increased injury risk in females. These multifaceted factors make women more susceptible to ACL tears compared to males.

Rehabilitation Protocol for Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) Reconstruction

General ACL recovery involves several key phases. Initially, patients focus on reducing pain and swelling. Subsequently, they work on regaining range of motion and strength through targeted exercises. As they progress, functional activities and sports-specific training are integrated. Patience, consistency, and adherence to a structured rehabilitation program are vital for successful recovery.

Torn Meniscus

Mincis, small muscle fibers in the knee, often tear when the ACL ruptures due to the sudden and forceful movement of the knee. As the ACL provides stability to the joint, its rupture causes instability, leading to the mincis getting stretched and sometimes torn in the process. This contributes to knee pain and dysfunction.
